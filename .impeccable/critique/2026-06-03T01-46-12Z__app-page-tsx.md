#### Anti-Patterns Verdict

This does not immediately look like a throwaway AI-generated UI. The restraint, small radii, plain Chinese product copy, and single-purpose dashboard structure are working. The AI tells show up in details: repeated card grid cadence, low-contrast gray labels, a colored side stripe in the explanation panel, and generic font handling.

**LLM assessment**: The biggest design risk is not spectacle; it is a quietly generic dashboard rhythm. Desktop is readable, but the first viewport spends a lot of space on eight similar cards before the core chart. Mobile becomes a long stacked feed before the user reaches the chart and explanation, which weakens the one-minute understanding goal from PRODUCT.md.

**Deterministic scan**: CLI scan of `app/page.tsx` returned clean. Directory-level scan of `app` found 2 warnings: `side-tab` at `app/globals.css:1037` and `overused-font` at `app/globals.css:40`.

**Visual overlays**: Browser injection succeeded on `http://localhost:3001`. The detector inserted 25 overlay nodes and reported low contrast labels, clipped positioned children inside relation cards, cramped chart SVG padding, the side-tab accent border, and single/overused Arial typography. This was captured in headless Chrome, so no persistent Human tab remains open.

#### Overall Impression

The interface is already usable and calmer than the common AI-dashboard default. Its single biggest opportunity is to become a sharper reader's instrument: less repeated card scanning, clearer current relationship focus, a stronger chart/explanation pairing, and mobile flow that gets to the insight faster.

#### What's Working

1. The product frame is right. Top cards, one main trend chart, one explanation panel, and a method disclosure match the actual job: understand a relationship, then inspect why it changed.
2. The visual restraint is a good base. White panels, gray canvas, and limited red/blue semantics avoid the intelligence-screen and finance-terminal anti-references.
3. The explanation panel has useful content density. Main event, summary, evidence lines, and reports are the right ingredients for trust.

#### Priority Issues

**[P1] Mobile path is too long before insight**

**Why it matters**: Casey, the distracted mobile user, must scroll past eight full cards before seeing the chart. That delays the primary task and makes the page feel like a list feed rather than a dashboard.

**Fix**: Convert featured pairs on mobile into a horizontal rail, compact segmented list, or smaller two-column summary where the selected pair stays visually anchored. Let the chart appear much earlier in the first mobile viewport.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable adapt app/page.tsx`

**[P1] Chart and explanation hierarchy needs a stronger reading path**

**Why it matters**: The core insight is split across score, line, highlighted segment, and explanation. On desktop this works but feels under-directed; on mobile the chart labels and segment annotations are too small to carry the story.

**Fix**: Make the selected relationship and current index the primary chart header, reduce low-value axis noise on mobile, improve selected segment callout placement, and align the explanation title/date/delta into a clearer first-read block.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable layout app/page.tsx`

**[P2] Accessibility and semantic color need tightening**

**Why it matters**: Browser overlays reported repeated `#9ca3af` low-contrast text on white. Red currently maps to high/friendly or improvement while blue maps to low/tension in places, which may be learnable but is not self-evident for general readers.

**Fix**: Darken secondary text where it carries meaning, pair every color-coded delta with text, standardize relationship color semantics in one token map, and add visible focus states for cards, tabs, selects, chart segments, and share/retry actions.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable audit app/page.tsx`

**[P2] Relation cards still have template-grid tells**

**Why it matters**: The repeated card anatomy is functional, but eight near-identical blocks create scanning fatigue and trigger the detector's clipped-overflow warnings. The `点击分析→` link-like label is also low-value repetition.

**Fix**: Give cards a denser information hierarchy: relationship, index, state text, delta, and sparkline with less repeated CTA copy. Remove unnecessary positioned separators or overflow clipping. Use active state as a strong but quiet selection marker.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable layout app/page.tsx`

**[P2] Explanation emphasis relies on a banned side stripe**

**Why it matters**: The `border-left: 3px solid ...` main-event treatment is exactly the kind of side-tab accent Impeccable flags as AI-ish. It also puts too much attention on decoration instead of source/date/evidence structure.

**Fix**: Replace the stripe with a full-width typographic emphasis, a subtle tinted evidence header, or a compact main-event row with icon/date/source treatment. Keep the main event prominent without a colored side rail.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable polish app/page.tsx`

#### Persona Red Flags

**Alex (Power User)**: Can complete the basic pair switch quickly on desktop using cards and selects. Red flags: no keyboard shortcut or command path for pair switching, no favorites/recent pairs, no compare mode, no export or saved view. This is acceptable for v1 but limits repeat analytical use.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent User)**: Red flags: low-contrast gray text appears in card captions and evidence metadata, focus styling is not consistently visible, chart segment meaning depends partly on color and small labels, SVG role/button interactions may not give enough accessible context beyond title text.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)**: Red flags: eight large cards push the chart far below the top, key chart labels are tiny, the share action appears low and small, and the explanation panel arrives after a long scroll. The page is readable but not thumb-efficient.

**Project-specific: Ordinary News Reader**: Red flags: relation color semantics need learning, method caveat is at the bottom, and the chart does not yet explain confidence or source limitations near the moment of interpretation.

#### Minor Observations

- The current brand mark is competent but a little generic; it can stay for now.
- The method section is strong, but mobile makes it feel like another card after a long stack.
- `最新数据` and selected chart date are close but not narratively tied; users may wonder why the page header and chart date differ.
- Report summaries appear only on hover/focus on desktop, which means mobile users may miss useful context.
- The favicon 404 seen in dev should be fixed eventually, though it is not a UI blocker.

#### Questions to Consider

- What if the selected relationship became the anchor and the other featured pairs became navigation, not full cards?
- Does every featured pair need a sparkline on mobile, or just current index/state/delta?
- Should relationship color semantics be intuitive to first-time readers, or explicitly taught with a compact legend near the chart?
- What would make the explanation panel feel like editorial evidence, not a generated summary block?
